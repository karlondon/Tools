## Installation Tips (UK-Specific)

### Before Buying:

1. **Check WiFi Signal:** Strong signal at front door is essential. Test with phone.

2. **Doorbell Requirement:** Some UK properties prohibit external modifications (flats, listed buildings). Check first.

3. **Weather Resistance:** All models rated for UK weather, but sheltered locations increase lifespan.

4. **Height:** Install at chest-to-head height (4-5 feet) for best face capture.

---

### Installation Steps:

1. **Charge battery fully** before installation
2. **Download app** and create account
3. **Mount bracket** to door frame/wall (screws or adhesive)
4. **Connect to WiFi** via app
5. **Attach doorbell** to bracket
6. **Test motion detection** and adjust zones
7. **Test doorbell press** and audio

**Time needed:** 15-30 minutes for most models.

## Common Questions (UK)

### Do I need planning permission?
**No** for most UK homes. Doorbells count as "permitted development." Exceptions: listed buildings, conservation areas, flats (need building consent).

### Will it work with my existing doorbell chime?
**Most can:** Ring and Nest offer plug-in chimes. Blink, Arlo, Eufy rely on phone notifications primarily.

### What about WiFi range?
**All require 2.4GHz WiFi** at door location. 5GHz not compatible. Most work up to 30 feet from router.

### Are they weatherproof?
**Yes:** All rated IP65 or better (UK rain, snow, heat). Sheltered location extends lifespan.

### Can thieves just steal the doorbell?
**Yes, technically:** But all have security features (tamper alerts, hardened screws). Theft is rare and covered by home insurance.

### Do they work during power cuts?
**Battery models: Yes** - Continue working on battery. Wired models: No power, no function.
