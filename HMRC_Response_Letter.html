Why There Is No P11D for 2025/26

With effect from 1 April 2025, my employer (Deloitte LLP) moved all taxable benefits — including my Private Medical Insurance (PMI) — to payrolling. This means the benefit-in-kind value has been added to my taxable pay each month and income tax collected via PAYE directly through my salary.

I enclose the following evidence to confirm this:

    • Line item: PMI BIK Taxable Credit — £201.02 (added to taxable pay each month)
    • The corresponding deduction removes this from my cash payment, confirming this is a benefit-in-kind taxed via payroll, not paid as cash

Because the tax on my PMI benefit has been collected monthly through payroll throughout 2025/26, no separate P11D should be expected or submitted for this tax year.

Important: Double Taxation — Request to Correct My Tax Code

I also wish to draw your attention to a significant issue with my current tax code.

Prior to payrolling, my PMI benefit was reported via P11D and my tax code was reduced accordingly to collect tax on the benefit. However, when payrolling commenced in April 2025, my tax code was not updated to remove the PMI adjustment.

This has resulted in double taxation throughout 2025/26:

  • The PMI benefit was taxed once via payrolling (by my employer, through PAYE each month)
  • The same PMI benefit was also taxed a second time via my reduced tax code (by HMRC, through a lower personal allowance)
