## The Double Taxation Problem

Since April 2025, you are being taxed on PMI **twice**:

1. **Via payroll** — Your employer adds the PMI benefit value to your taxable pay each month and deducts income tax on it through PAYE. ✅ *This is correct.*
2. **Via tax code** — HMRC has reduced your personal allowance to collect tax on PMI through a lower tax code. ❌ *This should have been removed when payrolling started.*

The overpayment = **your marginal tax rate × the PMI benefit value coded into the tax code**.

## How to Recover the Overpayment

1. **Chase HMRC** — Call 0300 200 3300 and reference your request to remove PMI from the tax code from 1 April 2025
2. **Request corrected tax code** — Should be **1257L** (standard) if PMI is the only adjustment, effective from April 2025
3. **P800 / tax calculation** — Once corrected, HMRC should issue a P800 for 2025/26 showing the refund, or adjust it through your 2026/27 tax code
4. **Check Personal Tax Account** — [gov.uk/personal-tax-account](https://www.gov.uk/personal-tax-account) for real-time updates
5. **Evidence to provide** — Your employer's confirmation that PMI has been payrolled since April 2025
