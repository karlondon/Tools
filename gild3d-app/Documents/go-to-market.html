Gild3d — Go-to-Market Strategy

Version: 1.0 Date: April 2026 Horizon: 90-Day Launch Plan Classification: Confidential
01   GTM Overview

Gild3d's go-to-market strategy is built on a controlled, quality-first launch that prioritises curated supply and targeted demand acquisition over mass-market volume — consistent with the platform's luxury positioning.

The platform will launch in three phases: a private soft launch with seeded companion profiles, a limited-access beta for invited members, and a public launch with active marketing. This sequencing ensures the platform delivers a premium first impression before opening to the broader market.

Core GTM Principle: Never open demand before supply is ready. A member who browses an empty platform will not return. Phase 1 launch requires a minimum of 15 published companion profiles before any member marketing begins.

04   Pricing Strategy

Pricing reflects the luxury positioning of the platform. Tiers are priced to create clear value differentiation and incentivise migration to higher tiers over time.

TierPriceAccessTargetUpgrade Trigger
FREE $0 Browse published profiles, no booking or messaging Prospective members evaluating the platform Finding a companion they want to connect with
SILVER $49/month Messaging, standard profile access, basic booking Occasional, price-conscious clients Wanting priority access or exclusive companions
GOLD $99/month Priority booking, extended companion media, no wait times Regular clients, business travellers Wanting VIP companions or private media access
PLATINUM $199/month Full access — VIP companions, private media, concierge priority High-frequency, high-value clients Already at top tier — retention focus

Pricing psychology: The FREE tier is a deliberate acquisition tool, not a revenue stream. Members who browse and find compelling profiles will upgrade. The jump from SILVER ($49) to GOLD ($99) is intentionally small to drive tier migration. PLATINUM at $199 should feel exclusive but not prohibitive for the target demographic. Review pricing at 200 active members using actual tier distribution data.

1

Supply Seeding

  • Onboard 15–25 companion profiles via admin
  • Professional photography / profile content
  • Quality review of all profiles before publish
  • Configure NOWPayments IPN webhook
  • Set up automated database backups
  • Internal UAT — full booking flow end-to-end
  • Legal review of TOS and privacy policy
  • Configure transactional email (production SMTP)
2

Private Beta
Weeks 4–8 (Controlled Access)
  • Invite 20–50 founding members (personal network, trusted referrals)
  • Collect qualitative feedback on UX and booking flow
  • Monitor payment conversion and drop-off
  • Identify and fix any booking flow friction
  • Test messaging and booking notification flows
  • Build first testimonials / social proof
  • Soft-launch SEO content and landing pages
3
Phase Three
Public Launch
Week 9+ (Full Acquisition)
  • Open registration to all with verified email
  • Activate paid and organic marketing channels
  • Launch referral incentive for founding members
  • PR outreach to niche press and communities

  • Scale companion supply alongside member growth
  • Weekly admin review of bookings and payments
  • Quarterly pricing and tier strategy review
06   Marketing Channels
🔍

SEO & Content

Long-form content targeting high-intent, low-competition keywords in the luxury companionship niche. Blog, landing pages, city-specific companion guides.

High ROI · Slow ramp
💬

Community & Forums

Authentic participation in privacy, crypto and luxury lifestyle communities (Reddit, Telegram). Referral links and tasteful promotion where permitted.

High targeting · Manual effort
🔗

Referral Programme

Founding members receive a shareable referral code. Successful referrals earn one month of membership upgrade. Viral loop with built-in trust signal.

Low cost · Trust amplifier
🌐

Niche Directories

Listing on relevant industry directories and review platforms where the target demographic searches. Focus on quality placement over volume.

Passive acquisition · One-time effort
✉️

Email Marketing

Capture email on landing page pre-launch. Nurture sequence for free tier members to convert to paid. Monthly newsletter for engagement retention.

High LTV impact · Owned channel
🤝

Agency & Network Partnerships

Partner with existing companion agencies to onboard their talent as verified Gild3d profiles. Commission-sharing or white-label arrangement possible.

Supply acceleration · Partnership required
📱

Private Social Media

Curated private Instagram / Twitter (X) accounts showcasing companion aesthetics and platform lifestyle. Drives aspirational brand awareness without mass exposure.

Brand building · Compliance sensitive
📰

PR & Niche Press

Outreach to crypto-lifestyle press, privacy technology publications and luxury lifestyle media. Positioning as the premium, crypto-native alternative.

Brand authority · Low cost
🎯

Crypto Community Advertising

Targeted advertising on crypto-native platforms (CoinGecko, crypto newsletters, Telegram channels). Reaches privacy-conscious, crypto-literate target demographic directly.

High relevance · Premium CPM

Note on traditional paid ads: Google Ads, Meta and mainstream platforms are unlikely to approve campaigns in this category. Budget allocated to traditional paid ads should instead be directed to crypto-native advertising and SEO content. This is a feature, not a limitation — it keeps customer acquisition costs low and reduces platform dependency on third-party ad networks.

07   90-Day Launch Roadmap
WEEKS 1–2Platform Readiness
  • Configure and verify automated database backups (critical — before any live data)
  • Set NOWPayments IPN webhook to production URL, test full payment cycle
  • Legal counsel review of Terms of Service and Privacy Policy for primary markets
  • Configure production SMTP with a managed provider (SendGrid or Postmark recommended)
  • Full end-to-end user journey test: register → browse → book → pay → confirm
  • Admin walkthrough session — Super Admin trained on dashboard, companion management, audit tools
WEEKS 3–4Supply Build
  • Onboard minimum 15 companion profiles (target: 20–25)
  • Professional photography or high-quality profile images for all companions
  • Complete profile details: bio, rates, location, services, languages, availability types
  • Set VIP and Featured flags for 3–5 top-tier companions
  • Publish profiles and conduct final QA review
  • Begin SEO content — 3 city guide pages, 2 blog posts targeting niche keywords
WEEKS 5–7Private Beta
  • Invite 20–50 founding members via personal network and direct outreach
  • Provide founding members with GOLD or PLATINUM trial period (1 month)
  • Collect structured feedback via short survey after first booking
  • Monitor booking completion rate, payment conversion rate and drop-off points
  • Address any UX friction points identified during beta
  • Build first 5–10 member testimonials (anonymous, with permission)
WEEKS 8–9Pre-Launch Marketing
  • Activate referral programme — founding members receive unique referral codes
  • Begin community presence in 3–5 relevant forums / Telegram groups
  • Submit platform to niche directories and review sites
  • Publish 2 additional SEO articles, begin link-building outreach
  • Configure email capture on homepage for pre-launch interest list
  • Brief crypto community advertising — test 2–3 ad creatives
WEEKS 10–12Public Launch & Scale
  • Open registration publicly — monitor server performance and error logs
  • Activate email nurture sequence for new FREE tier registrations
  • Weekly KPI review: registrations, free→paid conversion, booking volume, revenue
  • Scale companion supply to match member growth (target ratio: 1 companion per 10 active members)
  • PR outreach to 5 niche publications / newsletters
  • Month 3 retrospective — assess pricing, tier distribution, acquisition cost by channel

10   Launch Risks & Contingency
RiskLikelihoodContingency
Low beta member engagement — no bookings made during beta Medium Extend beta by 2 weeks. Review companion profile quality and pricing. Consider offering founding members first booking fee waived.
Companion supply too thin at launch — members can't find suitable profiles Medium Delay public launch until minimum 20 profiles are live. Prioritise supply over demand in early weeks.
Payment flow friction — members drop out at crypto payment step Medium Add clearer crypto payment instructions and tutorial video on checkout page. Consider stablecoin (USDT) as default currency option.
Community marketing backfire — negative reception in forums Low Approach all community marketing authentically — no spam. If reception is negative, pause that channel and focus on SEO and referral instead.
NOWPayments API instability affecting payments Low Monitor NOWPayments status page. Identify backup crypto payment processor (CoinGate, BitPay) for emergency fallover if needed.
Free-to-paid conversion significantly below 35% target Medium Review what access FREE tier provides — consider further restricting to make SILVER upgrade more compelling. Add exit-intent upgrade prompt.
11   Definition of Launch Success

Month 1 (Beta Complete): 20+ published companion profiles, 30 beta members active, at least 3 successful end-to-end bookings completed including crypto payment. No critical bugs or payment failures.

Month 3 (Post-Launch): 60+ paid members, $5,000+ MRR, 15+ bookings in the month, CAC below $80. Platform is operationally stable and generating revenue above operating costs.

Month 6 (Scale): 150+ paid members, $15,000+ MRR, 40+ bookings per month, 3+ companion profiles added during the period. Phase 2 features (password reset, booking notifications) scoped and funded from operating revenue.
