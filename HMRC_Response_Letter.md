**Re: Request for P11D Benefits Information — Tax Year Ended 5 April 2026**

Thank you for your letter dated 7 April 2026 requesting details of my job expenses and benefits from Deloitte LLP for the tax year ended 5 April 2026.

I am writing to confirm that **no P11D will be issued by my employer for the 2025/26 tax year**, and to bring to your attention an important matter relating to my tax code.

---

**Why There Is No P11D for 2025/26**

With effect from **1 April 2025**, my employer (Deloitte LLP) moved all taxable benefits — including my Private Medical Insurance (PMI) — to **payrolling**. This means the benefit-in-kind value has been added to my taxable pay each month and income tax collected via PAYE directly through my salary.

I enclose the following evidence to confirm this:

1. **Email/letter from my employer (Deloitte LLP)** confirming:
   *"From April 2025 these benefits were payroll'ed through your salary so therefore the tax on them was paid each month and will not be reported separately on a P11D for the 25/26 tax year."*

2. **My payslip for March 2026**, which clearly shows the PMI benefit being payrolled:
   - Line item: *PMI BIK Taxable Credit — £201.02* (added to taxable pay each month)
   - The corresponding deduction then removes this from my cash payment, confirming this is a benefit-in-kind being taxed via payroll, not paid as cash

Because the tax on my PMI benefit has been collected monthly through payroll throughout 2025/26, **no separate P11D should be expected or submitted** for this tax year.

---

**Important: Double Taxation — Request to Correct My Tax Code**

I also wish to draw your attention to a significant issue with my current tax code.

Prior to payrolling, my PMI benefit was reported via P11D and the tax code was reduced accordingly to collect tax on the benefit. However, when payrolling commenced in April 2025, **my tax code was not updated to remove the PMI adjustment**.

This has resulted in **double taxation** throughout 2025/26:

- The PMI benefit was taxed once via payrolling (by my employer, through PAYE each month)
- The same PMI benefit was also taxed a second time via my reduced tax code (by HMRC, through a lower personal allowance)

The impact:

| Item | Amount |
|------|--------|
| PMI benefit value in tax year 2025/26 | £2,082 |
| Tax rate (I am a higher rate taxpayer) | 40% |
| **Estimated tax overpaid due to double taxation** | **£832.80** |

I note that my current tax code for 2026/27 is **1015L** (personal allowance £10,150), whereas the standard allowance is £12,570 (code 1257L). The reduction of £2,420 closely matches my current annual PMI premium of £2,412, indicating that HMRC still has the PMI coded into my tax code — despite it being payrolled.

---

**What I Am Requesting**

1. **Confirmation** that no P11D is required from my employer for 2025/26, as benefits have been payrolled
2. **Correction of my tax code** to **1257L** with effect from **6 April 2025**, removing the PMI from the tax code as it is already taxed via payrolling
3. **Refund of overpaid tax** of approximately £832.80 for the 2025/26 tax year, arising from the double taxation described above
